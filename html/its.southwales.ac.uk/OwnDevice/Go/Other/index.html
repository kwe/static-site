Getting started with other devices

Welcome to the getting started guide for your device

(ie, other than Apple OS, Apple iOS, Android, Windows OS or WP7)

According to Moore’s law, the average capabilities of a computer can be doubled every 12 to 18 months. As a result the computer solutions available for users are changing and increasing all the time, meaning that despite all attempts to the contrary, it is not always possible to provide direct support for all devices all of the time.

Should you require access to our services using a device which has not yet been covered in one of our more in depth guides, provided below are the basic details you will require in order to connect to our services, which we hope will enable you to follow the manufacturer instructions provided with your device.

Connecting to the Wireless internet service.

In order to connect to our Eduroam Wireless internet service, ensure you are in a wifi enabled area and then browse for network. Treforest and Glyntaff wifi areas are displayed on our campus wireless map.

The network you need to connect to should be listed as Eduroam.

Other information you may require to configure the connection includes:

  • EAP method : PEAP
  • Phase-2 authentication : MSCHAPV2
  • CA Certificate : (unspecified)
  • User Certificate : (unspecified)
  • Identity :
    • If your University student ID/enrolment number starts with 13,12,11 then please enter your University student ID number followed by glam.ac.uk ** If your University student ID/enrolment number starts with 200 or 100 then please enter your University student ID number followed by newport.ac.uk
  • Anonymous Identity : Blank
  • Password : Please enter your current University password* Identity :
    • If your University student ID/enrolment number starts with 13,12,11 then please enter your University student ID number followed by glam.ac.uk ** If your University student ID/enrolment number starts with 200 or 100 then please enter your University student ID number followed by newport.ac.uk
  • Anonymous Identity : Blank
  • Password : Please enter your current University password

Once you have entered these details, click connect to connect to the service. You should now find that you are connected and able to browse the internet or access your emails without any problems.

In order to access the Eduroam network you must ensure that you have activated your university IT account and agree to be bound by our IT Services terms and conditions (PDF) whilst using our services.

IT account activation can be performed via the Self-service Account Administration (SSAA) webpage: https://ssaa.southwales.ac.uk

  • Note: This guide assumes that your wireless adapter has already been installed and is known to be working. It is the responsibility of the user to configure and manage their own wireless equipment

Connecting to your e-mail account.

Should you have a mobile device that is not covered by any of our previous guides, the raw details required for connecting to your e-mail account are as follows:

Connection type is IMAP, and your login details are your current University username and password.

Incoming Server:

Server Address imap.gmail.com
Port 993
Security type SSL (always)

Outgoing server:

Server Address smtp.gmail.com
Port 465
Security type SSL (always)
