Getting Started with your Windows Mobile device

Welcome to the getting started guide for your Windows Mobile device.

Below is a step by step guide which will walk you through the process of connecting to the Eduroam wireless service, on to configuring access to your email accounts and on to printing your work directly from your device (where available)

Connecting to the Wireless internet service.

The Eduroam service provides you with internet access on wireless network connections for your own equipment.

The service is now available at a number of University of South Wales locations, as displayed on our campus wireless map

  • Note: The example devices in the following guide are Windows Mobile 7.5 (Mango), but the instructions provided should be similar to those required for use with other versions of Windows Mobile 7.5 (Mango).

If you would like to connect a computer with other operating systems, please contact IT Services Customer Support, or see our other Eduroam guides.

  • Note: This guide assumes that your wireless adapter has already been installed and is known to be working. It is the responsibility of the user to configure and manage their own wireless equipment

Step 1/3 – Before you start…

In order to access the Eduroam network you must ensure that you have activated your university IT account and agree to be bound by our IT service terms and conditions whilst using our services.
IT account activation can be performed via the SSAA webpage: https://ssaa.southwales.ac.uk

Step 2/3 – Connecting your device to the Eduroam network.

Locate a suitable area where a wireless signal can be received (see the campus wireless map for details).

Locate and load up your devices settings, via the setting icon in on the home screen.

Select Wifi from the list.

Wait for your device to scan the available networks at your location, and then select Eduroam from the list of available options.

Step 3 of 3 – Network authentication

The device will now prompt you to enter your University username and password.

Username: Please enter your University student id number followed by glam.ac.uk if you id starts 13,12,11 or newport.ac.uk if your enrolment number starts 200 or 100
Password: Enter your current University password

Once you have entered your details, click done to commence connecting to the network.

When your device has connected correctly, you should be returned to the wifi settings menu, where the Eduroam network should display as connected.

Your device should now be configured successfully for use with the Eduroam wireless network, in
order to start using the service, simply close the settings menu’s, and open your preferred web browser.

Configuring access to your email accounts on your Windows Mobile device.

To use your University email on your mobile device, you first need to configure the device to connect
to the University email servers.

  • Note: The example devices in the following guide are Windows Mobile 7.5 (Mango), but the instructions provided should be similar to those required for use with other versions of Windows Mobile 7.5 (Mango).

As the University student email is run by Google, configuring the University email system is identical to
setting up a Gmail account.

Before proceeding to configure your device, please ensure that it is currently connected to the internet either via WiFi, or by any 3g service offered by your mobile network operator, as your device will need to be able to connect to Gmail in order to complete the process successfully.

Once you have connected, on your Windows Mobile device, open the Settings pane on your device and then select the ‘Email + Accounts’ option, as shown below:

And then select “Add an Email account” from the list of options.

You will now be prompted to choose what kind of email account to add. Select “Google” account from the available options.

You will now be prompted to enter their email address and password, please enter your
full University email address, i.e: 12345678@glam.ac.uk in the email box, and your current University
password in the password box, upon completion – and then click on the ‘Sign In’ button.

Your mobile device will now sign in/verify your email account details and then close the wizard returning you to the home screen.

On here you should find that a new menu option, labelled Gmail, has been created to provide you with direct access to your University account.

Your University email account should now be configured on your device, which should now begin syncronising your device with your accounts.
