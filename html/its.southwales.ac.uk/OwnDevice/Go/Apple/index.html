Getting started with your Apple Laptop

Welcome to the getting started guide for your Mac OS laptop.

Below is a step by step guide which will walk you through the process of connecting to the Eduroam wireless service, on to configuring access to your e-mail accounts and on to printing your work directly from your device (where available)

Connecting to the Wireless internet service.

The Eduroam service provides you with internet access on wireless network connections for your own equipment.

The service is now available at a number of University of South Wales locations, as displayed on our campus wireless map

This guide will show you how to connect to the Eduroam wireless network using a desktop computer or Macbook with Macintosh OS X.

If you would like to connect a computer with other operating systems, please contact IT Services Support , or see our other Eduroam guides .

  • Note: The example devices in the following guide are Macintosh OS X (10.7.3), but the instructions provided should be similar to those required for use with other versions of Mac OS X.

Step 1 of 3 – Before you start…

In order to access the Eduroam network you must ensure that you have activated your university IT account and agree to be bound by our IT service terms and conditions whilst using our services.
IT account activation can be performed via the SSAA webpage: https://ssaa.southwales.ac.uk

  • Note: This guide assumes that your wireless adapter has already been installed and is known to be working. It is the responsibility of the user to configure and manage their own wireless equipment

Step 2 of 3 – Setting Up OS X Macintosh for use with Eduroam

Locate a suitable area where a wireless signal can be received (see campus wireless map for full details).

After Macintosh OS X loads you should see your desktop, wait for Macintosh OS X to finish loading all
startup items.

At this point, ensure that the wireless adapter is switched on.

Access the ‘Airport’ wireless configuration by moving your mouse cursor over the Airport icon in the
apple menu and selecting it once.

A list of available wireless networks should now be listed, if in an appropriate area Eduroam should be listed, move your mouse cursor and select the Eduroam entry, after which the computer should initiate the connection process.

Step 3 of 3 – Network authentication

The computer will now prompt you to enter your University username and password, after entering
your University username and password, please select the ‘OK’ button, this will initiate joining the
Eduroam wireless network.

Username: Please enter your University student id number followed by glam.ac.uk if you id starts 13,12,11 or newport.ac.uk if your enrolment number starts 200 or 100.
Password: Enter your current University password
802.1X: Automatic

The first time you connect to the Eduroam network, you will be prompted to accept the server
certificate, select ‘Continue’ to accept.

As you will be making changes to the computer, you may be asked to enter an Administrator
password, upon entering the password in the password box click ‘OK’ to continue.

Your device should now be configured successfully for use with the Eduroam wireless network, in
order to start using the service, please load up Safari or your favourite web browser.

Configuring access to your email accounts on your Mac OS device.

Introduction

The University of South Wales offers all enrolled students the use of a free personal e-mail account which up until now has only been available via Glamlife, but can now be accessed using your
Macintosh e-mail client.

Your e-mail account includes a calendar, notes and an address book to organise and store your contacts, and provides more than 6Gb of storage space.

Step 1 of 1 – Setting Up Your Mac OS X computer

To use your University e-mail on your Macintosh, you first need to configure the device to connect to
the University e-mail servers.

As the University student email is run by Google, configuring the University email system is similar to
setting up a Gmail account.

The example pictures in the guide below are Macintosh OS X (10.7.3), but can be easily configured
for use with other devices as long as they support the IMAP email protocol.

On your OS X computer, locate and load up the ‘Mail’ application.

You will now be prompted to enter your full name, your University email address, and your current
University password, select ‘Continue’ to proceed.

Upon selecting ‘Continue’, the mail application will search for mail servers associated with your
University email address.

You will now be prompted to enter the incoming mail server details, please enter the following information.
Account type: IMAP
Description: UoG Email (or something similar)
Incoming mail server: imap.gmail.com
Username: Enter your University email address (i.e: 12345678@glam.ac.uk)
Password: Enter your current University password.

Select ‘Continue’ to proceed.

You will now be prompted to enter the outgoing mail server details, please enter the following information.
Description: UoG Email (or something similar)
Outgoing mail server: smtp.gmail.com
Use Authentication: Place ‘Tick’ in box
Username: Enter your University email address (i.e: 12345678@glam.ac.uk)
Password: Enter your current University password.
