SBPOG Project Requests

The Strategic Business Process Owners Group (SBPOG) combines the former VCEB ICT Sub-group and the Core Business Process Review Programme Board, and will oversee projects relating to the core processes and their underpinning ICT across the institution.

University of South Wales staff who wish to submit an Information and Systems project request can do so via the following five steps:

Step 1 — Submit the request

A fully supported SBPOG Project Request Form must be submitted to the SBPOG Request Mailbox no later than two weeks prior to the relevant meeting date.

For assistance in completing the Request Form, please see Guidance

Requests graded as either a Level 1 or a Level 2 project will not be accepted without a supporting Business Case.

Please liaise with one of the three VCEB ICT leads as listed below for assistance when scoping your project request. You may either contact them directly or via the SBPOG Request Mailbox..

Only correctly scoped requests accompanied by the project matrix form and, if appropriate, the business case will be submitted to SBPOG. Any requests received after the submission deadline will not be taken forward but instead be carried over to the next SBPOG meeting.

Step 2 — The request is processed

The request will be categorised into the most relevant area of Information and Systems, the three areas are:

  • Corporate and Business Systems – ICT Lead: Kevin Evans, Chief Technical Officer, IT Services
  • Learning, Teaching and Research – ICT Lead: TBA
  • Infrastructure – ICT Lead: Tony Evans, Head of Infrastructure, IT Services

Once categorised the request will be allocated a reference number and submitted for discussion at the SBPOG meeting.

Step 3 — SBPOG meeting: the request is considered

During the SBPOG meeting all requests will be discussed using key criteria contained within the request form, such as the amount of IT Services time required, funding requirements and benefit to the University, to determine whether or not the request should be sanctioned.

Strong bids will be measured against how successfully they meet the University’s Key Strategic Priorities and the ICT Strategic Priorities.

Step 4 — After the SBPOG meeting

Following the meeting, all proposers will be contacted to advise them of the decision. If successful they will receive a ‘Request Agreement’ advising them of the reference number, the project level, the agreed funding amount, the IT Services time allocation and the appropriate contact name. The request will then remain within the priority list awaiting implementation by PAB. If a request is unsuccessful, proposers will be advised of the reason that it was not agreed and invited to submit a revised request if they wish to.

Step 5 — Project Implementation

Requests held within the priority list are implemented as soon as the required resources become available. IT Services will liaise with proposers to agree a start date for their request in advance of the resources being allocated. Further information regarding the process that is followed once requests are in progress can be found on the Project Assurance Board page.

To find out more, browse the Strategic Projects FAQs.
