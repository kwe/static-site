Blended / Distant Learning Support

The Blended Learning Support team provides training, advice and guidance to academic colleagues in the pedagogical application of appropriate technologies for use within the teaching and learning process.

Primarily, we work on Strategic Business Process Owners Group (SBPOG)-approved projects . In particular we offer support in the development of Awards that can be delivered totally at a distance.

The level of support we offer can be tailored to match your requirements, ranging from consultancy to a much more ‘hands on’ approach. We adopt a multi-disciplinary team approach and so will work closely with colleagues from the Library and IT Services in order to help you create a solution which is most appropriate to your student audience.

Throughout the development process we can also provide full project management service in order to help ensure your project is delivered to agreed deadlines. Ultimately, any Award created will need to be delivered and maintained. We ensure that throughout the development phase that you are given ample opportunity to take ownership of your own development and are aware of what you will need to do in preparation for delivery to your students.

A Recent Project

Most recently the team has worked with academic colleagues from the Faculty of Advanced Technology to produce the MSc Sustainable Business Risk Management. This project, which is due to be completed shortly, has successfully been run on a face to face basis for 20 years. From September 2010 it will be available to students based anywhere around the world.

An Academic’s Viewpoint

Here is a quote from Dr Trevor Price, who at the time of working on the above project worked full time as a Senior Lecturer in the Faculty of Advanced Technology. Trevor has subsequently achieved the role of Head of Technology Enhanced Learning in CELT and now divides his time between both jobs.

Working with the Technology Enhanced Learning Support team provided constructively critical commentary on my professional practice – especially in developing blended learning materials, and other teaching, learning and assessment content that I had little previous experience in crafting. Having one’s academic output minutely scrutinised by an Instructional Developer and an Editor really added value, not just as it enhanced my professional practice, but also polished the aesthetic quality of the materials presented to our learners. It made me think critically about my teaching, learning and assessment approach.

Working closely with LRC colleagues meant that I had plenty of support in terms of copyright and trawling through information sources in order to finalise my online reading list. Access to multimedia developers and the support of media services meant that I could include some things I just simply couldn’t have created myself – e.g. interactive graphical elements and professional-looking films.
